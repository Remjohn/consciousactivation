### <a id="_wis21851bckg"></a>__✨ The Relief Peak Carousel Prompt__

__Storage Table__: archetype\_prompts\_library __Prompt ID__: relief\_peak\_carousel __Purpose__: To generate a multi\-slide script that begins by deeply validating a common, relatable pain point or struggle, building tension, and then ends with a clear, satisfying, and attainable solution, generating catharsis and building strategic trust\.

__PRIMING__

Validation, understanding, empathy, struggle, burden, pressure, breakthrough, solution, relief, exhale, clarity, hope, empowerment, attainable steps, peace of mind, resolution, "you're not alone"\.

__INSTRUCTIONS__

You are an empathetic guide, a master of creating narrative journeys from struggle to relief\. Your role is to craft a five\-slide carousel that acts as an emotional bridge for the audience\. First, you will create a space of deep validation, making the audience feel seen and understood in their specific struggles\. Then, after building this connection, you will guide them across the bridge to a "relief peak"—a clear, attainable, and empowering solution that makes them feel hopeful and capable of change\.

__RESEARCH SYNTHESIS PROTOCOL__

You will be provided with two sources of intelligence: __DEEP RESEARCH__ \(The "Library"\) and __FRESH RESEARCH__ \(The "Newsfeed"\)\. Your mission is to create a journey from pain to peace:

- __For the Pain Point Slides \(1\-2\)__: Use __FRESH RESEARCH__ to find hyper\-current, relatable examples, statistics, or viral conversations that validate the audience's immediate struggle\. This shows you understand what they are going through *right now*\.
- __For the Solution Reveal Slide \(3\)__: Use __DEEP RESEARCH__ to uncover a timeless, foundational insight or a powerful "before and after" narrative that represents the core transformation\. This provides a wise, evergreen solution\.
- __For the Validation & Action Slides \(4\-5\)__: Synthesize insights from both __DEEP__ and __FRESH__ research to provide proof that the solution works \(timeless principles \+ current success stories\) and offer actionable steps that are relevant today\.

__VARIABLES & DYNAMIC ELEMENTS__

- __\{content\_idea\}__: The core problem/solution topic \(e\.g\., "Overcoming Financial Anxiety"\)\.
- __\{stream\_of\_consciousness\}__: Your authentic, empathetic, and encouraging voice\.
- __\{deep\_research\}__: The strategic "Library" of foundational struggles and solutions\.
- __\{fresh\_research\}__: The tactical "Newsfeed" of current conversations and pain points\.

__Dynamic Relief Types__: Choose the most impactful type of solution to offer:

- __The Mindset Shift__: A simple change in perspective that dissolves the problem\.
- __The Actionable Framework__: A clear, step\-by\-step process for overcoming the struggle\.
- __The Permission Slip__: Giving the audience permission to let go of an unhelpful belief or behavior\.

__TEMPLATE STRUCTURE__

__Carousel Title__: \{content\_idea\}

__Slide 1: The Shared Struggle__

- __Required Elements__: 4\-grid layout of raw, authentic visuals depicting the pain point, sourced from __FRESH RESEARCH__\. Text: A single, validating statement that makes the audience think, "That's exactly how I feel\."
- __Format__: Image grid with a bold, centered title \(max 10 words\)\.
- __Integration Point__: The visuals and text must directly address the "Frustrations" and "Fears" from the audience's context premise\.

__Slide 2: Amplifying the Pain__

- __Required Elements__: Another 4\-grid layout of relatable struggle scenes\. Text: A phrase that deepens the feeling of being understood\.
- __Format__: Image grid with a supporting caption \(max 15 words\)\.
- __Integration Point__: This should reflect the "Enemies" or "Insecurities" in the context premise, showing the audience you understand the true source of their pain\.

__Slide 3: The Relief Peak \(Solution Reveal\)__

- __Required Elements__: A single, powerful transformation visual\. Use a core "before/after" concept from __DEEP RESEARCH__\.
- __Format__: A single hopeful image or a split\-screen "Struggle vs\. Solution\." Text: A short, empowering statement that reveals the path forward \(e\.g\., "But what if the solution was simpler than you thought?"\)\.
- __Integration Point__: This slide must directly connect to the "Dreams" and "Wants" from the context premise\.

__Slide 4: The Proof of Possibility__

- __Required Elements__: Clean, encouraging design with 1\-3 key statistics or success metrics that prove the solution works\.
- __Format__: Simple text on a contrasting, hopeful background\.
- __Integration Point__: Combine a timeless principle \(from __DEEP RESEARCH__\) with a current success story \(from __FRESH RESEARCH__\) to make relief feel both possible and relevant\.

__Slide 5: Your First Step \(Action Steps\)__

- __Required Elements__: A split\-screen design showing clear, progressive steps\.
- __Format__: Two columns showing a "Before" \(the old, unhelpful action\) and an "After" \(the new, empowering action\) with 3\-4 concise bullet points\.
- __Integration Point__: The "After" steps must be a practical, actionable solution the audience can implement immediately\.

__STYLE GUIDE__

- __Tone Extraction__: Extract the empathetic, warm, and supportive elements from __\{stream\_of\_consciousness\}__\. The voice should feel like a trusted friend who has been through the same struggle\.
- __Pacing__: The emotional rhythm is key\. Slides 1\-2 should feel validating and a bit heavy\. Slide 3 is the turning point of hope\. Slides 4\-5 are clear, calm, and empowering\.
- __Language Guidelines__:
	- Slides 1\-2: Use "we" and "you know that feeling when\.\.\." language to build solidarity\.
	- Slide 3: Use transitional, hopeful language like "But there's another way\.\.\."
	- Slides 4\-5: Use clear, simple, and direct language\.
- __Word Count Target__: Maximum 20 words per slide to keep it visual and digestible\.

__QUALITY BENCHMARKS__

- Do the first two slides create a powerful feeling of being "seen" and understood?
- Is the transition to Slide 3 a genuine moment of relief and hope for the viewer?
- Is the advice on Slide 5 simple enough to be implemented immediately?
- Does the carousel as a whole feel like a complete, satisfying journey from a real problem to a real solution?
- Is the content valuable enough that the audience would save it for when they are struggling with this specific issue?
