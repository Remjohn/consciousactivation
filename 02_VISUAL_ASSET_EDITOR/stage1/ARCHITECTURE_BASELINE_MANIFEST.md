# Stage 1 Architecture Baseline Manifest

Generated: 2026-07-14

## Baseline status

This checkout is a PRD/governance package, not an implemented architecture. The binding baseline is therefore documentary and contractual, with no executable service, storage, runtime, UI, worker, or deployment baseline present.

## Preserved upstream architecture

- PRES-001 - Three compilation targets: Atomic Content Harness, Visual Asset Editor, and Content ↔ Asset Delegation remain independent products.
- PRES-002 - Canonical Harness IR: Harness IR remains the Builder's machine-readable source of truth; the Visual Production Plan IR is editor-local and cannot replace it.
- PRES-003 - Capability Ownership Map: Capabilities retain explicit ownership among code, typed model programs, JIT Skills, references, humans, evaluators, adapters, and hybrid pipelines.
- PRES-004 - Phase and Context Graphs: Phase-local context, progressive disclosure, and output-only downstream handoffs remain binding.
- PRES-005 - Typed Contract Graph: Cross-phase and cross-product handoffs remain typed, versioned, validated, and non-mutable downstream.
- PRES-006 - Canonical Skill Ecology: Canonical Skills, harness adaptations, composition recipes, and JIT Execution Capsules remain separate layers.
- PRES-007 - Deterministic JIT compiler: Critical capsule and context assembly remains deterministic and receipted.
- PRES-008 - Reference and Loading Graph: Doctrine, registries, examples, and SPR resources remain versioned and phase-local.
- PRES-009 - Dependency and Precedence Resolver: Runtime bindings resolve authority, conflicts, missing data, invalidation, and degradation before execution.
- PRES-010 - Behavioral evaluation and maturity: Skills, adaptations, recipes, capsules, and generated systems earn production maturity through evidence.
- PRES-011 - Builder Workflow Runtime: Human, agent, and deterministic-code actors remain explicit in typed event-sourced workflows.
- PRES-012 - Harness Control Tower: The Visual Editor extends the event-sourced Control Tower and does not create a disconnected authority store.
- PRES-013 - Repair and Invalidation Graph: Smallest responsible repair, downstream invalidation, frozen valid state, regression, and receipts remain binding.
- PRES-014 - Implementation Authorization Gate: Readiness is evidence-backed authorization, not document completeness.
- PRES-015 - Development Capsule: Implementation receives a traceable, authorized package rather than speculative code or boilerplate.
- PRES-016 - Four category constitutions: Short-Form Edited Video, 2D Character Animation, Carousels, and Supervisuals remain distinct governed categories.
- PRES-017 - Content Harness semantic authority: The requesting harness owns meaning, Activative purpose, sequence role, and final composition intent.
- PRES-018 - Activative Sequencing Intelligence: The Visual Editor supplies assets and geometry to category/format sequencing; it does not replace sequencing authority.

## Binding prohibitions

- AG-001 (hard_gate): The product does not implement or redesign the Atomic Harness Builder.
- AG-002 (hard_gate): The product does not own Content Harness meaning, Activative policy, sequence role, or final composition authority.
- AG-003 (hard_gate): The product does not use ComfyUI JSON as the canonical product specification.
- AG-004 (hard_gate): The product does not route all work through one general visual-editor agent.
- AG-005 (hard_gate): The product does not require the same fixed specialist chain for every request.
- AG-006 (hard_gate): The product does not include a standalone Rights Analyst or routine manual rights-review layer.
- AG-007 (hard_gate): The product does not treat editing as proof that provenance is unnecessary.
- AG-008 (hard_gate): The product does not let production models approve their own output.
- AG-009 (hard_gate): The product does not accept the first passing or fastest candidate by default.
- AG-010 (hard_gate): The product does not run blind retries without a causal binding or route change.
- AG-011 (hard_gate): The product does not exceed three autonomous quality-repair rounds.
- AG-012 (hard_gate): The product does not judge visual fatigue from usage frequency alone.
- AG-013 (hard_gate): The product does not use OKF, a vector database, or embeddings as canonical operational truth.
- AG-014 (hard_gate): The product does not load the entire knowledge corpus into every model context.
- AG-015 (hard_gate): The product does not turn a single successful candidate into a production Steering Recipe.
- AG-016 (hard_gate): The product does not train a LoRA because one normal demand failed.
- AG-017 (hard_gate): The product does not let high Budget Programs become uncontrolled random generation.
- AG-018 (hard_gate): The product does not silently relax an accepted Visual Asset Demand.
- AG-019 (hard_gate): The product does not claim production support for structurally represented but uncertified asset families.
- AG-020 (hard_gate): The product does not require routine manual ComfyUI graph, prompt, seed, LoRA, or mask manipulation.
- AG-021 (hard_gate): The product does not allow in-flight runs to adopt unpinned capability or runtime updates.
- AG-022 (hard_gate): The product does not allow a local product release to change category constitutions or shared Activative doctrine.
- AG-023 (hard_gate): The product does not substitute a beautiful asset for failed composition effectiveness.
- AG-024 (hard_gate): The product does not authorize implementation because the PRD is complete.
