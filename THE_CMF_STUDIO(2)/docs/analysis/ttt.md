That last part — transition patterns — is where the system gets genuinely sophisticated.

**Step 4 — Negative Space Per Level**

Each client has things they won't do even at extreme temperatures. Extract these explicitly from the interview — moments where they pull back, soften unexpectedly, or reframe. These are the edges of their range and they're as defining as the examples.

## The Revised Architecture
```
TTT Framework
     │
     ├── Identity Anchors (invariant across all 9 levels)
     │   → vocabulary, metaphor domain, reader relationship
     │   → what they never do regardless of temperature
     │
     ├── Level Signatures (client-specific expression of each level)
     │   → not the archetype — this client's version
     │
     ├── Transition Grammar (how they move between levels)
     │   → what typically precedes/follows each level for this client
     │
     └── Negative Constraints (per level limits)
         → what they won't do even at TTT-09
```

The generator uses identity anchors as permanent constraints, level signatures as the style target, and transition grammar to make multi-beat pieces feel coherent rather than stitched.
