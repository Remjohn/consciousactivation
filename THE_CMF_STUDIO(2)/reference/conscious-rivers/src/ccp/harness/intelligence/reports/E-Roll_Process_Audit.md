**Date:** January 30, 2026
**Author:** CMF Visual Pipeline Audit
**Scope:** E-Roll Research Planning, Query Generation, and Report Quality
**Word Count:** ~1600

---

## Executive Summary

The E-Roll procurement process suffers from a critical **execution gap**: the methodology is sound, but the reports bypass it. The `🔮 E-Roll Research Planning Generator.md` describes a rigorous 5-phase workflow with Deep Cultural Research. However, actual project reports (Fitou, Monia) use **generic stock photo keywords** that produce 85-90% unusable results.

**The Core Problem:** Agents generate queries that would work on Shutterstock, not queries that embody Conscious Movie Alchemy principles.

---

## 1. What the Methodology Prescribes (The Guide)

The `E-Roll Research Planning Generator.md` is well-designed. It mandates:

### The 5-Phase Locked Workflow

1. **Input Gathering:** Read script, Tribe Soul Profile, Brand Avatar.
2. **12 Introspection Questions:** Answer about THE CULTURE, not the script.
3. **Deep Research (Browser):** Find REAL, VERIFIABLE cultural references.
4. **Deep Research Report:** Document NAMED references with URLs.
5. **Query Generation:** Each query cites its source from the Deep Research Report.

### The Key Safeguard

> [!CAUTION]
> **DO NOT GENERATE SEARCH QUERIES WITHOUT A DEEP RESEARCH REPORT.**
> Generic queries like "ladder climbing success" or "breakthrough celebration" are FORBIDDEN.

### The Quality Standard

| ❌ WRONG (Generic) | ✅ RIGHT (Culturally Specific) |
|---|---|
| "ladder climbing success" | "Maison Château Rouge Youssouf Fofana Bogolan" |
| "herbal cleanse detox" | "Kinkeliba thé longue vie African morning ritual" |
| "breakthrough celebration" | "S'enjailler celebration Ivorian diaspora France" |

**The Guide is clear:** Queries must be culturally specific, not stock-photo generic.

## 3. Root Cause Analysis

### 3.1 The Deep Research Phase is Skipped

The 5-phase workflow mandates browser-based research to find NAMED cultural references. The actual reports show no evidence of this research. There are no:
- Named heroes/icons
- Documented cultural events
- Verified source URLs
- Specific cultural terms beyond generic "African woman"

**Impact:** Without Deep Research, queries default to stock-photo language.

### 3.2 Queries Describe Emotions, Not Objects

Conscious Movie Alchemy principle: **"Show the specific to evoke the universal."**

| Emotion-Based Query (Bad) | Object-Based Query (Good) |
|---|---|
| "Energy surging through woman" | "Steam rising from cup of Kinkeliba tea, hands wrapped around ceramic" |
| "Burst of light abstract gold" | "Morning sun through dusty window, construction site visible outside" |
| "Knife stabbing abstract red" | "Close-up of palm pressing into side, fingers digging into fabric" |

**The Fix:** Queries should describe **what the camera would see**, not **what the viewer should feel**.

### 3.3 Queries Use AI Art Vocabulary

Many queries use terms that AI image generators respond to but that don't exist in stock photography databases:
- "Glowing aura"
- "Abstract gold particles"
- "Vitality energy surging"
- "DNA merging with textiles"

**The Problem:** Pexels, Unsplash, and Google Images don't index "aura" or "vitality energy." These queries return zero results or irrelevant stock.

## 4. The Real Purpose of E-Roll (Clarification)

### 4.1 What E-Roll is NOT For

E-Roll is **NOT** for sourcing:
- Generic faces (AI generates these better)
- "50-year-old West African man bald glasses" → We generate this with CAC
- Random stock photography of emotions
- Abstract CGI or metaphorical imagery

**If AI can generate it, E-Roll should not source it.**

### 4.2 What E-Roll IS For

E-Roll exists to source **what AI cannot generate**:

| Category | What to Source | Why AI Cannot Generate This |
|---|---|---|
| **Recognizable Celebrities/Influencers** | Crazy Sally, Mrs Roots, Fatou Guinea, Tatiane Van Laethem | AI cannot create a recognizable face that the audience already knows. |
| **Real-Life Contextual Clues** | Kinkeliba tea leaves in a wooden bowl, Bogolan mudcloth texture, actual hibiscus flowers | AI gets these details wrong. Real objects have authenticity. |
| **Cultural Icons & Symbols** | Wax print patterns, specific African textiles, Maison Château Rouge imagery | These are copyrighted/specific. AI approximates but doesn't replicate. |
| **Nostalgic Aesthetics** | Actual 1990s Abidjan street photography, genuine archival images of "Maman Africaine" figures | AI cannot recreate authentic time-period aesthetics. |
| **Documentary/Archival Footage** | France Inter articles about "Syndrome Méditerranéen," medical bias documentation | This is journalism, not generation. |

## 5. The Fix: Query Reformation Protocol

### 5.1 The Object-First Rule

Every query must start with a **physical object** or **observable action**, not an emotion.

| Before (Emotion-First) | After (Object-First) |
|---|---|
| "Exhaustion fatigue tired" | "Woman with head rested on arms at table, empty plate nearby" |
| "Energy vitality power" | "Steam rising from cup, morning light through window" |
| "Pain suffering agony" | "Hand pressing into side, fingers gripping fabric" |

### 5.2 The Cultural Specificity Layer

After the object, add cultural markers from the Deep Research phase:

| Generic Object | Culturally Specific |
|---|---|
| "Herbal tea" | "Kinkeliba tea in ceramic cup, dried leaves visible" |
| "African woman" | "Ghanaian woman, Kente print head wrap, gold earrings" |
| "Construction site" | "French urban construction, scaffolding, bricks stacked" |

### 5.3 The Light/Time Anchor

Specify the light condition to ensure results match the emotional register:

| Emotion | Light Anchor |
|---|---|
| Despair, Fatigue | "Dim room, tungsten light, evening" |
| Hope, Clarity | "Morning sun, soft golden hour" |
| Tension, Conflict | "Harsh midday, high contrast shadows" |

## 6. Recommendations

### Immediate Actions

1. **Enforce Deep Research Phase:** No query generation without a completed `[project]_ERoll_Deep_Research_Report.md`.
2. **Reject Emotion-Only Queries:** Any query without a physical object should be flagged and rewritten.
3. **Ban AI Art Vocabulary:** "Glowing," "aura," "particles," "abstract" should trigger automatic rejection.

### Structural Changes

1. **Query Validation Checklist:** Add to E-Roll Commander:
   - [ ] Does query contain a physical object?
   - [ ] Is query culturally specific (not "African woman" but "Ivorian woman, wax print")?
   - [ ] Does query specify light condition?
   - [ ] Is query free of AI art vocabulary?

2. **Audrey as Template:** Use the Audrey E-Roll Report as the quality benchmark. It demonstrates the correct approach.

3. **Cross-Reference Visual Schema:** E-Roll queries should pull from the same Visual Schema used by CAC and Storyboard. Location anchors, object anchors, and cultural symbols should appear in E-Roll queries.

---

## 7. Conclusion

The E-Roll methodology is sound. The Guide correctly mandates Deep Cultural Research and forbids generic queries. The failure is in execution: agents skip the research phase and generate queries optimized for AI image generators rather than stock photo databases.

**The 85-90% discard rate is not a filter problem—it's a query problem.**

When queries describe **observable objects** in **culturally specific contexts** with **explicit light conditions**, the hit rate will dramatically improve. The Audrey report proves this is achievable. The path forward is enforcement, not redesign.
