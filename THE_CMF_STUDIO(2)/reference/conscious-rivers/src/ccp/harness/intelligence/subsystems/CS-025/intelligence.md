# CS-025 - Retention Cascade

CS-025 treats the first 3 seconds as a survival gate for everything else in the video. The core insight is simple: once a viewer survives the earliest scroll window, the odds of continued watch improve sharply. That makes the HOOK a compression problem, not an introduction problem.

The subsystem reads time-to-face, time-to-stakes, HOOK ASL, focal clarity, and opening CLS. It succeeds when the strongest signal arrives fast, the cut rhythm is tight without becoming cognitively expensive, and the opening proves relevance before it asks for patience. It fails when the opener uses ceremony, delay, logos, or clutter.

In CMF, the HOOK should feel like a sharp admission into meaning, not a preamble. This subsystem belongs in the survival tier with CS-001 because if the opening leaks attention, every later subsystem becomes irrelevant.

## Current CMF Thresholds

- retention-critical window: first 3 seconds
- target HOOK ASL: 0.8 to 1.5 seconds
- opening CLS: max 2
- time to face: ideally inside 2 seconds
- time to strongest signal or stakes: inside the initial decision window

## Good Example

The coach appears immediately with a charged problem statement and clear focal structure.

## Failure Example

The opener spends 2 seconds on brand atmosphere and then begins a generic explanatory setup.
