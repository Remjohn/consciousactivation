# CS-023 - Rhythm Generator

CS-023 exists because viewers respond better to timing patterns that mix local bursts with long-range variation than to evenly spaced or arbitrarily scattered cuts. In CMF, rhythm should therefore be generated with structure rather than guessed ad hoc.

The subsystem reads runtime, target ASL, BPM, scene sequence, and pacing constraints. It succeeds when the timing field feels organic and narratively aligned. It fails when the sequence becomes robotic or chaotic.

CS-023 is a baseline timing subsystem. It creates the temporal field that other systems such as CS-024 and CS-015 can then tighten or slow according to cognitive conditions.

## Current CMF Thresholds

- use 1/f-inspired duration distribution
- require reset spaces in the timing map
- target mobile-appropriate ASL band: 0.8 to 2.4 seconds

## Good Example

The HOOK clusters short bursts, SETUP steadies, and TURNING_POINT tightens again without becoming metronomic.

## Failure Example

Every cut arrives at nearly the same interval, making the pacing feel synthetic and predictable.
