# CS-011 - Peak-End Budget Allocator

CS-011 exists because retrospective memory is dominated by the peak and the ending rather than by average quality across the whole piece. In CMF, that means equal treatment of every scene is a misallocation. Some beats carry structure, but two beats carry memory value.

The subsystem reads arc position, predicted climax strength, ending design, and available complexity budget. It succeeds when the turning point and the end receive disproportionate compositional force. It fails when the middle of the video is polished evenly while the key remembered moments are underbuilt.

CS-011 is a recall-tier subsystem. It should guide effect budget, graphics density, sound emphasis, and even human review time. If a tradeoff is necessary, preserve peak and end first.

## Current CMF Thresholds

- default peak budget multiplier: 2.0x
- default end budget multiplier: 2.0x
- middle-scene treatment: standard unless explicitly escalated

## Good Example

The revelation and the final future-state beat receive the strongest graphic and score lift, while setup scenes remain clean and functional.

## Failure Example

The exposition sequence is over-produced and the ending lands as a generic CTA card.
