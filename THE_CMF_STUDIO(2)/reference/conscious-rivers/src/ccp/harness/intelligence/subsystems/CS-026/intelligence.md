# CS-026 - Identity Tracking

CS-026 exists because each added face creates identity bookkeeping cost. In CMF, that means extra people should not be introduced casually just to add surface variety.

The subsystem reads face count over time, role necessity, shot prominence, and whether secondary faces are functional or decorative. It succeeds when the social field stays simple enough that attention remains on meaning. It fails when needless faces create a tax on comprehension.

CS-026 also supports authenticity because one continuous recognisable coach face often increases processing fluency and trust.

## Current CMF Thresholds

- one dominant coach face per video by default
- decorative secondary faces: 0
- allow secondary faces only when narrative role is explicit

## Good Example

The sequence holds one dominant coach face and only introduces a second face for direct testimony.

## Failure Example

A teaching sequence cuts to multiple random faces for atmosphere and forces unnecessary individuation.
