# CS-029 - ISC Quality Scorer

CS-029 exists to estimate whole-composition synchrony quality from the subsystem outputs CMF already produces. Direct ISC measurement is too expensive for runtime, but the conditions that increase synchrony are known: structure, focal discipline, continuity, pacing coherence, emotional clarity, and retention survival.

The subsystem reads the outputs of active subsystems and converts them into a synthetic score for publishability and review priority. It succeeds when CMF can explain whole-piece quality rather than celebrating isolated craft wins. It fails when local improvements hide global weakness.

CS-029 should be the final quality layer before commit or render. It is especially important during regeneration, where a beat may improve in isolation while the full composition becomes less coherent.

## Current CMF Thresholds

- publish-ready ISC quality score: 80
- revise band: 65 to 79
- high-risk band: below 65

## Good Example

A composition passes structure, retention, pacing, and binding checks and receives a high composite synchrony score before render.

## Failure Example

The revised beat looks better locally, but the full-sequence score drops because pacing and continuity now conflict.
