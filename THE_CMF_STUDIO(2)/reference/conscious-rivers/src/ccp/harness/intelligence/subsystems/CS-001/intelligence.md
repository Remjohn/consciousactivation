# CS-001 - First Frame Imprint

CS-001 is the survival gate for the rest of the composition. The subsystem exists because mobile viewers do not grant a reflective first impression; they grant a biological sorting decision. In the first 400 milliseconds the visual system decides whether the frame is legible enough to admit into attention or noisy enough to reject. That means the opening frame cannot carry branding vanity, ornamental motion, or stacked explanation. It must present one dominant perceptual answer immediately.

For CMF, the job is simple: create one high-confidence focal anchor in the upper-central zone with low initial complexity. The subsystem reads first-frame saliency, focal count, placement, and initial CLS. It succeeds when the viewer can lock on one thing without searching. It fails when there are multiple competing anchors, low-contrast clutter, or caption piles that turn admission into work.

This subsystem should run before CS-002 and CS-025 because it determines whether the HOOK even earns the chance to use faces or retention compression. A good opening frame feels inevitable and obvious. A bad opening frame feels like the viewer arrived late to a messy layout.

## Current CMF Thresholds

- imprint window: 400 ms
- focal anchors allowed: exactly 1
- opening CLS: max 2
- preferred dominant-element vertical band: 15% to 40%
- competing text stacks: not allowed

## Good Example

A coach face or one startling object appears against clean negative space, above center, with no extra labels.

## Failure Example

A logo animation opens over low-contrast footage while captions and a lower-third compete for attention.
