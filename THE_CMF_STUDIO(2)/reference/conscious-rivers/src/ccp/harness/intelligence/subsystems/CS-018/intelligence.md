# CS-018 - Presence/Arousal

CS-018 exists because strong motion is not automatically immersive. In CMF, the better question is whether a movement effect increases felt presence more than it increases arousal cost.

The subsystem reads predicted presence, predicted arousal, CLS burden, and scene role. It succeeds when motion helps the viewer feel inside the scene. It fails when the effect mainly creates agitation or self-conscious stylization.

CS-018 is a guardrail against over-editing and a way to formalize why subtle drift often beats dramatic camera turbulence.

## Current CMF Thresholds

- minimum presence/arousal ratio: 1.25
- high arousal motion reserved for TURNING_POINT and VISION
- standard CLS motion budget: 2 or less outside peaks

## Good Example

A breathing micro-drift supports confession and keeps the viewer in the emotional space.

## Failure Example

A restless shake effect makes the beat feel hectic instead of embodied.
