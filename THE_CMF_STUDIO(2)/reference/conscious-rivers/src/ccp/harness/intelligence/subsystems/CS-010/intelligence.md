# CS-010 - Cross-Modal Audio Primer

CS-010 exists because music and leading audio can shape interpretation before the eye has fully evaluated the image. In CMF, sound should often establish the emotional field first, especially around vulnerability, recognition, and turning points.

The subsystem reads audio key, mode, timbre, onset timing, scene role, and transition type. It succeeds when sound prepares the next beat emotionally before the image lands. It fails when audio arrives late, contradicts the target feeling, or forces the image to carry the entire emotional burden.

CS-010 makes J-cuts an explicit runtime decision rather than an editor taste preference.

## Current CMF Thresholds

- J-cuts preferred for emotional transitions
- audio preload lead: 150 to 500 ms
- avoid contradictory mode shifts across sensitive transitions

## Good Example

A low-register swell begins before the silent reaction shot appears, so the image lands inside an already-formed emotional field.

## Failure Example

The score changes only after the visual asks for interpretation, making the beat feel emotionally late and overworked.
