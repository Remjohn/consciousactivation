# CS-003 - Gaze Direction Transfer

CS-003 exists because faces can either monopolize attention or transfer it. Direct gaze strengthens social connection, while averted gaze can create joint attention and route the viewer into text, diagrams, or product proof.

The subsystem reads eye-line direction, overlay position, scene purpose, text density, and graphic timing. It succeeds when gaze semantics match the scene job. It fails when the face competes with the message or points attention in the wrong direction.

CS-003 is most valuable in proof, instructional, and CTA-adjacent scenes where the face should recruit attention first and then hand it off cleanly.

## Current CMF Thresholds

- direct gaze for bonding scenes
- averted gaze for instructional transfer scenes
- maximum gaze-overlay mismatch: 25 degrees
- no dense instructional overlay with strong direct gaze unless staged sequentially

## Good Example

The coach glances screen-right as a keyword card appears in the same direction, making the overlay feel socially authorized.

## Failure Example

The coach stares into camera while a dense diagram appears off-axis, forcing the viewer to split loyalty between face and explanation.
