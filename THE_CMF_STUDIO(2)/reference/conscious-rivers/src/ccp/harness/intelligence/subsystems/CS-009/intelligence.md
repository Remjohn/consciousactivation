# CS-009 - Emotional Contamination

CS-009 exists because neutral images can inherit emotional meaning from the sequence around them. In CMF, that lets the system use emotionally absorptive B-roll instead of chasing expensive literal illustration.

The subsystem reads the leading emotional vector, B-roll neutrality, contradiction risk, and sequence length. It succeeds when the insert supports the affective field without overpowering it. It fails when the insert is so semantically loaded that it hijacks the emotional inference.

CS-009 is especially useful in coaching and proof sequences where simple footage can become meaningful if the surrounding field is set correctly.

## Current CMF Thresholds

- contamination window: 3 to 5 short shots
- maximum contradiction score: 0.3
- prefer neutral, compositionally strong B-roll over emotionally noisy literal footage

## Good Example

A quiet architectural insert absorbs the grief established by the coach and score.

## Failure Example

A highly specific stock scene starts telling its own emotional story and breaks the intended inference.
