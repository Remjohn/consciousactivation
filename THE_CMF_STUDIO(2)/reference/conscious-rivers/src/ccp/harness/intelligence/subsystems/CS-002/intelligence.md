# CS-002 - Face Recognition Window

CS-002 exists because faces are processed by specialized recognition pathways and can hold attention faster than most synthetic layout tricks. In CMF, that makes the coach face a structural retention tool, not just a stylistic option.

The subsystem reads face count, framing scale, visibility of eyes and mouth, vertical placement, and time-to-face. It succeeds when a single usable face appears early enough to serve as a social anchor. It fails when the face is delayed, too small, visually obscured, or multiplied without narrative reason.

CS-002 is especially important in HOOK, recognition, and direct teaching scenes where the system needs to buy enough time for language, captions, or proof overlays to work.

## Current CMF Thresholds

- recognition window: 1.5 to 2.5 seconds
- preferred time to face: within 2.0 seconds
- preferred face count: 1
- preferred face top band: 15% to 25% from top
- eyes and mouth must remain visible

## Good Example

A medium close-up coach face appears immediately in the upper third and holds attention into the first teaching sentence.

## Failure Example

The scene opens on abstract atmosphere, delays the face, and asks the viewer to decide relevance without a social anchor.
