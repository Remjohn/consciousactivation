# CS-033 - Prediction Error Gate

CS-033 exists because attention is not driven by arousal alone. The viewer keeps an active model of what should happen next, and the composition earns attention when it violates that model just enough to force an update. In CMF, that makes prediction error a budgeted resource rather than an accidental byproduct of style.

The subsystem reads the active container's prediction error budget, the chosen component's surprise profile, reveal timing, and the legibility cost created by the schema break. It succeeds when the beat produces a meaningful expectancy violation that remains resolvable inside the scene. It fails when the beat is either too obvious to wake attention or too chaotic to decode.

CS-033 should usually coordinate with HOOK, CHALLENGE, and TURNING_POINT decisions, where novelty and contrast often do the most structural work. It is equally useful during regeneration, where a technically cleaner revision can accidentally erase the very surprise event that made the beat watchable.

## Current CMF Thresholds

- minimum effective prediction error score: 0.35
- maximum tolerated legibility loss: 0.25
- preferred reveal window: 250 ms to 2200 ms after beat admission
- unresolved surprise should settle within 3.0 seconds

## Good Example

The hook opens on an unexpected contrast or claim, but the viewer can resolve its meaning within the same beat.

## Failure Example

The sequence uses novelty for novelty's sake, creating confusion that steals attention from the message instead of sharpening it.
