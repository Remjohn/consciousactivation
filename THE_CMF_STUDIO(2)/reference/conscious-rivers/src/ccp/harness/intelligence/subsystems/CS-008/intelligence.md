# CS-008 - Excitation Transfer Timer

CS-008 exists to convert residual arousal into emotional depth. After a high-intensity event, the body stays activated longer than the mind stays attached to the cause. That delay creates a short window where a quiet follow-up can absorb the remaining charge and feel disproportionately meaningful. CMF uses that window deliberately.

The subsystem reads the CLS timeline, beat durations, and transition spacing. It succeeds when a CLS 4 beat is followed by a CLS 1 or 2 beat within roughly 2 to 5 seconds. It fails when the follow-up arrives too soon in the same high-energy mode, or too late after the arousal has decayed. A CLS 4 followed by CLS 4 is not stronger structure. It is transfer failure.

CS-008 should be consulted any time the timeline is re-cut around the peak, because even a small edit can destroy the golden zone. When this subsystem fails, the usual recovery path is to insert or strengthen a silence container, a vulnerable hold, or another low-CLS receiving beat.

## Current CMF Thresholds

- transfer trigger: preceding beat at CLS 4
- acceptable receiving beat: CLS 1 to 2
- valid transfer window: 2 to 5 seconds
- full decay risk: 10 seconds or more
- forbidden adjacency: CLS 4 to CLS 4

## Good Example

A revelation hits hard, then the composition cuts into a quiet close-up or pause within the golden zone.

## Failure Example

A revelation is followed by another hype cut sequence, preventing re-labeling and flattening emotional impact.
