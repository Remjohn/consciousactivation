# CS-024 - Arousal-Pacing Gate

CS-024 exists to enforce a rule editors regularly violate: emotionally intense content usually needs slower editorial pacing, not faster. LC4MP implies that overload arrives sooner when arousing material is also cut too aggressively. If the scene is already hot, fast cutting often pushes the viewer out of understanding and into defensive processing.

The subsystem reads scene arousal, CLS, the current shot-duration map, and informational density per cut. It succeeds when high-arousal scenes are given longer holds and low-arousal explanatory scenes use speed only where the informational burden stays manageable. It fails when intensity and cut density compound each other into noise.

In CMF, this subsystem should be consulted before editors add extra cuts to “juice” a peak. Most of the time the right move is the opposite: hold longer, simplify the visual field, and let the emotional content do the work.

## Current CMF Thresholds

- high-arousal beat: CLS 3 to 4
- recommended hold for high-arousal beats: 3 to 5 seconds
- lower-arousal explanatory beats may use: 1.5 to 3 seconds
- overload risk increases when short durations are paired with high information-per-cut

## Good Example

After a hard truth lands, the reaction shot is held long enough to process emotion and language together.

## Failure Example

A peak beat is chopped into multiple rapid inserts, making the viewer process editing mechanics instead of the message.
