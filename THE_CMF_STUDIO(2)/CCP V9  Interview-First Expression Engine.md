CCP V9 changes the launch center of gravity.

**Most experts do not lack knowledge. They lack activation.**

* stories

* hard-earned credibility

What they lack is:

* activation  
* structure

* extraction

* asset multiplication

The new core loop is:

Activation → Articulation → Asset Creation → Narrative Memory → Product Expansion

The first product is not a full community, challenge, webinar, Power Hour, or book.

The first product is a guided human conversation that activates expression and turns it into usable assets.

The guest or client should not feel like they are joining software. They should feel like they are entering a meaningful conversation where their best ideas, stories, and emotional truths become clearer.

This creates a new operational doctrine:

**Do not ask people to join software before they have experienced being activated.**

The system starts with events, podcast appearances, roundtables, or guided solo interviews. These sessions generate authentic expression. The backend then extracts stories, beliefs, triggers, frameworks, emotional edges, and asset routes.

# **Narrative State Induction**

## **Definition**

Narrative State Induction is the process of using research, interviewer resonance, audience reality, Matrix of Edging, question architecture, and standardized response anchors to guide a guest into a specific expression state while preserving authentic human speech.

It is not manipulation.

It is not scripting.

It is not making the guest perform.

The objective is:

Bring out the guest’s clearest truth in the state most useful for identification, teaching, authority, vulnerability, story, humor, challenge, or invitation.

# **V. The Five Primary Expression States**

These are not the full content taxonomy. They are induction states.

The retained content archetype inventory remains the actual output taxonomy.

The five states are upstream expressive modes that help produce the raw material.

## **1\. Cinematic State**

Induced by questions about:

* place  
* image  
* atmosphere  
* sound  
* body memory  
* sensory detail  
* first visual memory

Best for:

* story clips  
* documentary openings  
* transformation stories  
* backstory reveals  
* cinematic shorts  
* book trailers  
* memory-based carousels

Example induction:

“When you go back to that night, what is the first image that comes back?”

## **2\. Vulnerability State**

Induced by questions about:

* silence  
* cost  
* fear  
* shame  
* grief  
* what was never said  
* what was carried alone  
* what the person could not explain at the time

Best for:

* identification clips  
* confessional turns  
* witness stories  
* community invitations  
* challenge prompts  
* emotional carousels

Example induction:

“What did you carry alone because saying it would have changed how people saw you?”

## **3\. Authority State**

Induced by questions about:

* principles  
* decisions  
* what the guest knows now  
* what people misunderstand  
* what changed their worldview  
* what they would correct publicly

Best for:

* thought leadership clips  
* ranked takes  
* challenger / frame breaker clips  
* authority proof stack  
* webinar sections  
* LinkedIn posts

Example induction:

“What do people misunderstand about identity when they talk about it too quickly?”

## **4\. Teaching State**

Induced by questions about:

* frameworks  
* steps  
* layers  
* mistakes  
* mechanisms  
* what people need to understand first

Best for:

* core educator clips  
* explainers  
* stepwise teaching listicles  
* webinar lessons  
* Power Hour teaching  
* carousel frameworks

Example induction:

“If you had to teach this to someone who has never lived it, what would they need to understand first?”

## **5\. Invitation State**

Induced by questions about:

* who needs to hear this  
* what conversation should begin  
* what action the viewer should take  
* what question the viewer should ask themselves  
* what community should gather around this idea

Best for:

* CTAs  
* polls  
* challenge invitations  
* community prompts  
* webinar closes  
* event invitations

Example induction:

“Who needs to hear this, and what conversation do you hope it gives them courage to start?”

# **VIII. Response Anchor System**

A Response Anchor is a standardized phrase or constraint that helps the guest begin or deepen an answer without scripting the answer.

There are two core anchor types:

1. First-Line Anchor  
2. Depth Anchor

Landing is not anchored.

Landing is evaluated.

---

# **IX. First-Line Anchor**

## **Definition**

A First-Line Anchor is a standardized opening phrase given by the interviewer that the guest starts with.

Its purpose is to control:

* clip start  
* hook  
* emotional keyword field  
* guest state  
* backend extraction boundary

The First-Line Anchor is mandatory for any answer intended to become a clean content unit.

## **Why It Matters**

The first two to three seconds determine:

* the viewer’s attention  
* the guest’s cognitive lane  
* the emotional vocabulary used  
* the clip boundary  
* the backend’s confidence in extraction

Without a First-Line Anchor, guests often begin with:

* “Yeah, I think…”  
* “It’s complicated…”  
* “You know…”  
* “In my opinion…”  
* “Historically…”

These starts often make clipping harder and induce explanation instead of expression.

With a First-Line Anchor, the guest enters the correct state immediately.

Example:

First-Line Anchor:  
“That night in Bwiza, I understood…”

This pushes the guest toward:

* memory  
* meaning  
* identity  
* consequence

Instead of:

* vague explanation  
* historical summary  
* generic reflection

---

# **X. Depth Anchor**

## **Definition**

A Depth Anchor is the cognitive pressure inserted into the question or follow-up to prevent shallow, centroid answers.

It is not necessarily a phrase the guest must repeat.

It is a constraint that steers the answer toward:

* cost  
* contradiction  
* emotional truth  
* identity shift  
* hidden tension  
* audience recognition  
* mechanism reveal

The Depth Anchor exists because a clean start is not enough.

A guest can start cleanly and still answer generically.

Example of weak output:

“That night in Bwiza, I understood that identity is complicated.”

The start is clean, but the answer is flat.

A Depth Anchor sharpens the field:

“What did it cost the young Claude to be seen as a category instead of a person?”

Now the guest is less likely to drift into generic commentary.

## **Depth Anchor Rule**

The Depth Anchor should constrain the emotional topology, not dictate the answer.

Bad Depth Anchor:

“End by saying identity is not given, it is chosen.”

Good Depth Anchor:

“What part of your identity did that moment try to take from you?”

The second one creates depth without scripting the landing.

---

# **XI. Landing Evaluation**

The landing should not be forced.

The system does not know in advance where the guest’s best answer will resolve.

Possible landing types:

* Principle Landing  
* Emotional Recognition Landing  
* Teaching Landing  
* Question Landing  
* Invitation Landing  
* Contradiction Landing  
* Unresolved Tension Landing  
* Silence / Open Wound Landing  
* Humor Landing  
* Warning Landing  
* Proof Landing

The backend evaluates whether the landing is usable.

If the landing is weak, a follow-up can be generated.

But the original answer is allowed to breathe.

This preserves authenticity.

# **XV. Interviewer Pre-Induction Session**

Before generating final questions, the agent should interview the interviewer for 10–15 minutes.

This is not optional in the mature workflow.

It creates the Inter­viewer Resonance Context.

## **Why It Exists**

A great interview is not extraction.

It is a human-to-human field.

Connection requires two people.

The agent must understand the guest, but it must also understand the interviewer’s live relationship to the guest’s story.

This is what AI cannot replace.

The agent prepares the conversation.

Then it prepares the interviewer.

Then the human creates the connection.

## **Recommended Sequence**

1\. Guest research  
2\. Audience reality research  
3\. Initial narrative state hypothesis  
4\. Agent interviews interviewer for 10–15 minutes  
5\. Interviewer Resonance Context generated  
6\. Question flow rewritten  
7\. Asset contracts finalized  
8\. Live session begins

## **Pre-Induction Questions for Interviewer**

Examples:

1. “Which scene or idea from the guest’s work stayed with you most?”  
2. “Where did you feel personally implicated?”  
3. “What part of their story do you understand emotionally?”  
4. “What part do you not understand yet?”  
5. “What audience are you holding in mind during this conversation?”  
6. “What would make this interview feel alive instead of literary?”  
7. “What question would you ask if there were no camera?”  
8. “Where should you share a small reflection to make the conversation mutual?”  
9. “What do you not want to fake?”  
10. “Which state should open the session: cinematic, vulnerability, authority, teaching, invitation?”

# **XXV. Evaluation Doctrine**

Every interview-derived asset should be evaluated.

## **Interview-Level Evals**

* Did the guest enter the intended expression state?  
* Did the First-Line Anchor create a clean start?  
* Did the Depth Anchor prevent a flat answer?  
* Did the answer preserve authenticity?  
* Did the answer avoid generic centroid language?  
* Did the answer produce a usable landing?  
* Did the answer generate asset routes?  
* Did the question feel conversational, not interrogative?

## **Asset-Level Evals**

* Is the clip self-contained enough?  
* Does the first 2–3 seconds hook?  
* Does the clip contain emotional or cognitive force?  
* Does the edge survive editing?  
* Does the asset match the intended archetype?  
* Is the visual treatment aligned with the emotional state?  
* Does it avoid generic AI aesthetics?  
* Does it preserve the guest’s dignity and truth?

## **Backend Learning**

The system should store:

* which anchors worked  
* which depth anchors produced emotional density  
* which archetypes fit each guest  
* which questions created flat responses  
* which edge products survived  
* which coalition signatures failed  
* which landings performed best

This becomes the future moat.

Expression states are upstream. Content archetypes determine output.

Each session improves:

* interviewer profile

* archetype fit
