# CCP Conscious Sequencing & Expression Acquisition Engine V1

## 1. Executive position

The engine is not a storytelling plug-in. It is the explicit architectural layer that connects pre-interview intelligence, interview design, expression capture, asset routing, composition, and publishing.

Storytelling organizes events. Archetypes organize meaning. Sequencing organizes the state transition of the guest, the viewer, and the audience relationship. Composition organizes perception. Rendering produces the phenotype.

The operational doctrine is:

```text
The Interview Brief shops.
The interview harvests.
The Expression Ingredient Inventory stores.
The Conscious Sequencing Engine cooks.
The Composition Engine plates.
CMF renders.
Evals taste.
```

The system must plan enough to acquire useful material while preserving the possibility that the guest reveals something better than the plan.

---

## 2. Why sequencing is a first-class system

CCP already contains:

- Brand Context and Voice / Visual DNA;
- Audience Reality and Context Premises;
- Interviewer Resonance;
- Matrix of Edging;
- Narrative State Induction;
- First-Line and Depth Anchors;
- Interview Asset Contracts;
- Complete Expression Sessions;
- Expression Moments;
- archetype and derivative registries;
- Complete Editing Sessions;
- CMF render routes;
- evaluation receipts.

What was missing was an explicit object answering:

> What raw ingredients must be acquired, and in what order should approved ingredients later be disclosed to the viewer?

Without this layer, the system risks conducting a rich interview that does not contain the necessary mechanism, proof, payoff, visual evidence, action, or invitation required by the intended assets.

## 3. Three sequencing scales

### 3.1 Interview-State Sequencing

The interviewer guides the guest through psychologically coherent states. A typical route may be:

```text
rapport
→ safety
→ authority
→ cinematic recall
→ vulnerability
→ meaning
→ teaching
→ invitation
```

The route is adaptive. The live assistant must never chase a missing ingredient at the cost of dignity, trust, or an authentic emotional peak.

### 3.2 Viewer-State Sequencing

The final asset may use these operational states:

```text
Perceptual Entry
→ Relevant Open Question
→ Active Prediction
→ Truthful Payoff
→ Human Affinity
→ Expected Future Value
```

The first four are primarily asset-level. Human Affinity spans the asset and relationship. Expected Future Value is mainly package- and series-level.

### 3.3 Package / Series Sequencing

A Guest Asset Pack or monthly publishing program should be ordered so that assets collectively build:

```text
recognition
→ human connection
→ concrete value
→ differentiated authority
→ participation
→ recurring value expectation
```

A package is not merely a set of ten unrelated deliverables.

---

## 4. Five sequencing dimensions

Every `ContentSequenceProgram` is evaluated across five dimensions.

### Semantic sequencing
The order in which claims, scenes, evidence, mechanisms, and conclusions become intelligible.

### Emotional sequencing
The order and amplitude of recognition, tension, vulnerability, relief, conviction, humor, and invitation.

### Curiosity sequencing
The opening, development, transformation, and closure of viewer questions.

### Composition sequencing
The order in which visual focal points, text, proof objects, diagrams, character reactions, and motion reveal information.

### Asset sequencing
The order in which published assets build a durable relationship and category expectation.

## 5. Expression ingredient system

### 5.1 Ingredient classes

#### Human-expression ingredients
Captured from authentic speech or nonverbal behavior:

- scene;
- sensory detail;
- emotional cost;
- contradiction;
- belief before and after;
- hidden mechanism;
- memorable phrase;
- natural humor;
- usable principle;
- audience invitation;
- pause or facial reaction.

#### Research and evidence ingredients
Sourced through CRAL, Guest Dossier, or verified external evidence:

- statistics;
- chronology;
- source quote;
- scientific evidence;
- comparison;
- proof object;
- cultural or historical context.

#### Audience-reality ingredients
Sourced from comments, debates, search language, and current audience pressure:

- wrong belief;
- recognizable objection;
- unresolved debate;
- identity tension;
- tribal phrase;
- familiar scenario.

#### Visual ingredients
Sourced or requested for composition:

- archival image;
- meaningful object;
- location;
- document;
- screenshot;
- timeline milestone;
- visual metaphor;
- Micro-Semiotic Anchor.

#### Brand-continuity ingredients
Retrieved from Brand Context:

- signature worldview;
- recurring metaphor;
- approved phrase;
- Voice DNA marker;
- Visual DNA motif;
- approved character / prop;
- recurring series promise.

### 5.2 Required versus optional ingredients

A sequence hypothesis declares ingredient requirements before the interview. Each requirement has:

- semantic role;
- preferred source;
- mandatory or optional status;
- minimum quality thresholds;
- acquisition instrument IDs;
- acceptable substitutions;
- fallback sources;
- supported asset routes.

### 5.3 Ingredient provenance

Every captured ingredient carries exact provenance:

```text
session ID
recording artifact
speaker
start/end timestamps
transcript text
source type
extraction program version
human edits
hash
```

No renderer receives unattributed semantic material.

## 6. Interview Brief V2 as procurement plan

The Interview Brief must contain more than research and questions. It includes:

```text
Research Context
Asset Portfolio Intent
Sequence Hypotheses
Expression Acquisition Plan
Interview-State Sequence
Interview Asset Contracts
Live Coverage Policy
Doctrine Constraints
```

### 6.1 Asset Portfolio Intent

Defines what the session is expected to support, such as:

- Cinematic Story Commentary;
- Educational Explainer;
- Challenger clip;
- Living Commentary Reaction;
- timeline carousel;
- conceptual contrast carousel;
- meme visual;
- poll visual;
- reaction seeds.

It is an intent, not a guarantee. The actual output portfolio is revised after ingredient quality is known.

### 6.2 Sequence Hypothesis

A provisional recipe that specifies:

- target archetype;
- derivative and format;
- likely viewer question;
- promised payoff;
- expected wrong model;
- closure type;
- affinity opportunity;
- future-value key;
- ingredient requirements.

The hypothesis guides procurement but may be replaced by a stronger sequence discovered during the session.

### 6.3 Expression Acquisition Plan

Aggregates ingredient requirements across all hypotheses, deduplicates shared needs, and maps them to:

- interview questions;
- First-Line Anchors;
- Depth Anchors;
- follow-up instruments;
- visual-asset requests;
- research tasks;
- pickup contingencies.

## 7. Interview Asset Contract V2

The atomic unit is not a question. It is an acquisition and routing contract.

Each contract defines:

- target expression states;
- question and anchors;
- edge product;
- ingredient targets;
- expected source material;
- sequence hypotheses served;
- asset routes;
- clip boundary rules;
- landing targets;
- repair follow-ups;
- coverage success rule;
- safety constraints.

A contract may intentionally acquire ingredients for several future assets.

## 8. Live Ingredient Coverage Tracker

The live assistant maintains a coverage matrix by sequence hypothesis and asset route.

Example:

```text
Cinematic Story Commentary
✓ clean first line
✓ location and sensory image
✓ disturbance
△ emotional cost too abstract
✕ turning point
✕ principle landing

Educational Explainer
✓ recognizable wrong belief
✓ lived anomaly
✕ hidden mechanism
✕ replacement model
△ action too generic
```

### 8.1 Cue policy

A live cue may recommend:

- ask for a scene;
- ask for cost;
- ask for body memory;
- request a concrete example;
- clarify the hidden mechanism;
- ask for an audience-facing rule;
- mark a timestamp;
- move to the next state.

### 8.2 Suppression policy

The assistant must suppress a cue when:

- the guest is in an emotional peak;
- the cue would break rapport;
- the ingredient can be acquired later;
- the guest has implicitly refused the territory;
- the question would become exploitative;
- a stronger unexpected ingredient is emerging.

The interface should show coverage without turning the interviewer into a checklist operator.

## 9. Post-session Expression Ingredient Inventory

After transcription and alignment, the extraction pipeline creates a durable pantry of reusable ingredients.

Each ingredient is scored for:

- specificity;
- emotional density;
- truth density;
- audience relevance;
- clipability;
- explanatory completeness;
- evidence strength;
- visual routeability;
- source integrity;
- brand fit.

The inventory also records:

- planned ingredients captured;
- planned ingredients missing;
- unexpected high-value ingredients;
- weak ingredients;
- substitute ingredients;
- required pickups;
- research gaps;
- visual asset requests.

### 9.1 Ingredient graph

Relationships between ingredients include:

```text
opens_question
adds_stake
provides_clue
complicates
contradicts
reframes
answers
provides_proof
humanizes
creates_future_value
```

The graph lets the compiler assemble source-grounded sequences without relying only on transcript adjacency.

## 11. Content Sequence Program

The final program is compiled after the ingredient inventory exists.

It contains:

- immutable context refs;
- selected pattern;
- viewer entry and target exit states;
- central question;
- promised payoff;
- open-loop and closure contracts;
- ordered beats;
- ingredient IDs per beat;
- primitive coalition per beat;
- composition function per beat;
- emotional curve;
- format adapter;
- affinity contract;
- future-value contract;
- doctrine constraints;
- evaluation requirements.

The LLM can propose the program. Pydantic validates it. Evals and operator approval freeze it. The renderer only executes frozen data.

## 12. Format adapters

### 12.1 Cinematic Story Commentary

Shopping list:

```text
clean opening line
location and time
sensory image
people present
disturbance
body response
emotional cost
turning point
meaning
memory object / archival cue
quiet human landing
```

Default sequence:

```text
Unusual image or emotional line
→ Unresolved scene
→ Sensory clues
→ Emotional or social cost
→ Turning point
→ Meaning reveal
→ Quiet human landing
```

### 12.2 Educational / Explainer

Shopping list:

```text
common wrong belief
why it persists
lived anomaly
counterexample
hidden mechanism
analogy
replacement model
practical application
warning or limitation
```

Default sequence:

```text
Unexpected metaphor
→ Counterintuitive question
→ Familiar wrong model
→ Anomaly or evidence
→ Hidden mechanism
→ Correct framework
→ Application
```

### 12.3 Living Commentary Reaction

Shopping list:

```text
proof object or quote
spontaneous first reaction
emotional pause
interpretation
personal resonance
nuance
judgment
audience-facing question
```

Default sequence:

```text
Proof object
→ Human reaction
→ Why it matters
→ Pause / unresolved tension
→ Interpretation
→ Validation or question
```

### 12.4 Conscious Reactions Editing

Shopping list:

```text
two legitimate positions
choice criteria
prediction
rankable items
verdict
unexpected reversal
reaction moment
defense of choice
audience vote prompt
```

Default sequence:

```text
Choice prompt
→ Audience prediction
→ Evidence / rounds
→ Score change or reversal
→ Reveal
→ Human reaction
→ Participation prompt
```

### 12.5 Carousel

Default state mapping:

```text
Slide 1: Perceptual Entry + Open Question
Slide 2: Stakes / relevance
Slide 3: Familiar model or clue
Slide 4: Contradiction
Slide 5: Reframe / payoff
Slide 6: Application or proof
Slide 7: Affinity / invitation / future value
```

The actual sequence must respect the selected carousel sequence grammar and composition registry.

### 12.6 Single-image post

A single image uses a compressed spatial sequence:

```text
focal object
→ headline or question
→ contrast / proof / metaphor
→ interpretation
→ brand signature
```

Its scene spec must include an explicit `attention_path`.

## 13. Package sequencing

A recommended Guest Asset Pack relationship sequence is:

1. Cinematic story — recognition and humanity.
2. Educational explainer — concrete value.
3. Challenger asset — differentiation.
4. Reaction asset — judgment and personality.
5. Timeline carousel — depth.
6. Contrast or relief carousel — reusable framework.
7. Micro-contradiction meme — compression and sharing.
8. Tribal-relief meme — familiarity.
9. Tension poll — participation.
10. Dilemma or future-question post — open the next conversation.

The package compiler should optimize for:

- emotional range;
- archetype diversity;
- route diversity;
- repeated-value coherence;
- fatigue avoidance;
- balanced authority and humanity.

## 14. Ethical sequencing doctrine

The engine optimizes earned attention, not compulsive attention.

### Allowed

- progressive disclosure;
- meaningful contrast;
- delayed explanation;
- evidence reversal;
- source-supported reframe;
- genuine unresolved questions;
- truthful suspense.

### Forbidden

- false hook claims;
- manufactured conflict;
- unclosed promised payoff;
- distortion of guest meaning;
- sensationalizing unresolved trauma;
- fabricated scarcity or urgency;
- generic “wait until the end” manipulation;
- using vulnerability as bait.

Human truth outranks recipe completion.
