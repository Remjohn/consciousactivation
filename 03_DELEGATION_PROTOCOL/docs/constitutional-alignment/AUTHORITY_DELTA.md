## Executive finding

V1.1 does not invalidate the existing protocol architecture, lifecycle, integrity,
idempotency, replay, audit, or persistence work. It closes a semantic-contract
gap in the Visual Asset Demand and strengthens the meaning of compatibility.
The local reference engine must be patched, not restarted.

The protocol remains a deterministic boundary service. It validates, preserves,
hashes, negotiates, and routes authoritative semantic objects; it does not compile
Activative Intelligence, choose recognition carriers, author visual narratives,
rank creative alternatives, or perform VAE production.

## Constitutional delta

### 1. Constitutional precedence

The embedded Constitution `1.1.0` is now the highest-order authority whenever a
Delegation artifact governs Activative or visual meaning. Sparse downstream
tokens are allowed only when the demand preserves stable references to the rich,
frozen upstream objects and their versions.

### 2. Constitution-complete semantic lineage

The Visual Asset Demand must preserve the rich upstream chain from Identity DNA,
Context Premise, resonance and Matrix of Edging through the Activative
Intelligence Pack, human reaction or viewer-role activation, Expression Moment,
format harness, final asset, and receipts.

The V1.1 demand introduces or elevates these protected domains:

- `activative_semantic_lineage`
- `activation_contract`
- `visual_semantic_pack`
- `visual_narrative_program`
- `feature_contracts`
- `somatic_route_request`
- `expression_moment_lineage`
- `wrong_reading_locks`

Reaction Receipt and Expression Moment references live within the semantic
lineage in the supplied V1.1 schema, while `expression_moment_lineage` is also
named as an authority domain. This path/cardinality mismatch requires the human
decision recorded as `HD-002`.

### 3. Activation Contract

The demand must carry the edge pressure, activation directions, viewer roles,
stance, identity urges, intended reaction, participation design, and
micro-commitment. The Content Harness owns these values. The protocol validates
and preserves them. The VAE enforces them without rewrite.

### 4. Visual Semantic Pack

The demand must preserve recognition intent, recognition carrier, audience
visual-world references, emotional-load carrier, and Semiotic MCDA receipt,
with optional visual dog whistles and real-life reference classes. The VAE may
execute the chosen carrier but may not reselect it.

### 5. Visual Narrative Program

The demand must preserve pattern match, pattern interrupt, attention-state
sequence, viewer-role progression, prediction gap, payoff, affinity field, and
anticipation residue. The VAE executes the program without reauthoring it.

### 6. Feature Contracts and somatic routing

Meaning-bearing features such as gaze, hands, posture, witness relationships,
object punctum, crop, and negative space require explicit contracts when they
determine meaning. T/V codes route somatic delivery only after the recognition
carrier and narrative job are known; they do not choose either.

### 7. Wrong-reading locks

`wrong_reading_locks` becomes mandatory and non-empty. A receiver that accepts
the field but cannot enforce or evaluate the required locks is incompatible.

### 8. Enforcement-based compatibility

JSON parseability is insufficient. `COMPATIBLE` requires behavioral support for
every mandatory semantic, authority, lifecycle, evaluation, profile, and failure
behavior. Adapters and migrations may rename, nest, or normalize fields, but may
not drop, weaken, synthesize, flatten, or reinterpret constitutional objects.

Lossless treatment is specifically required for:

- Identity DNA, Context Premise, resonance, Matrix of Edging, and Activative
  Intelligence Pack lineage;
- Reaction Receipt and Expression Moment references;
- activation directions, roles, stance, identity urges, participation design,
  intended reaction, and micro-commitment;
- recognition intent/carrier and the Visual Narrative attention program;
- feature contracts, T/V request, and wrong-reading locks.

### 9. Conformance expansion

Later executable conformance must prove:

1. an adapter that drops an Expression Moment reference is rejected;
2. a receiver that ignores required wrong-reading locks is incompatible;
3. migration preserves the version and hash lineage of the Activative
   Intelligence Pack;
4. VAE mutation of recognition intent, role, stance, or Visual Narrative
   Program is rejected without state change or production effect;
5. a declared evaluation profile that cannot evaluate the requested activation
   or feature contracts produces incompatibility.

## Local baseline differences that must be bridged

The local `1.0.0-rc.2` package is stricter than the PRD-level V1 schema in useful
ways: public objects are closed, references use exact version/hash/canonical-ref
identity, and every value path has one generated owner. Those protections must
remain.

The bridge is not a copy operation. Batch B must merge the V1.1 semantic objects
into the stronger local baseline and retain exact references, closed schemas,
canonical hashing, authority coverage, and immutable demand identity.

Known naming differences require an explicit V1-to-V1.1 migration:

| Local `1.0.0-rc.2` | V1.1 authority | Required treatment |
|---|---|---|
| `activative_intent` | `activation_contract` plus existing `activative_function` semantics | lossless canonical migration; no inferred fields |
| `wrongness_locks` | `wrong_reading_locks` | lossless rename with non-empty enforcement |
| `composition` | `composition_intent` | retain the strict local geometry model under the canonical V1.1 name or an explicitly ratified equivalent |
| bare capability strings | field/behavior/evaluator enforcement support | expand compatibility evidence; parsing is not support |
