# Relevant ADRs

- `ADR-001` keeps Builder compilation and handoff inside the modular-monolith boundary and stops at compiled packages and Development Capsules. SHA-256: `ac021e5b1567922e56ecf644e420d9ce2bc3f36d21ee46c8fbdb7b0a3ca27531`.
- `ADR-004` requires deterministic compilation, immutable versions, canonical serialization, replay, and governed compatibility. SHA-256: `e2cd894d1db6f165c4952cd7126924d1a6761e3c232f1dbd5e4e120dc8fcb71b`.
- `ADR-005` requires explicit human, agent, code, and external authority boundaries. SHA-256: `783835d294590b52737f841adf59f588bce41bbae4fd3d2e0b7ddcc425aaf92e`.
- `ADR-012` requires deny-by-default least privilege; this Story emits scope and authority, not an implementation sandbox. SHA-256: `0c8c0d0e9dff8ce1ec9a61069946b1d3998bc870c1c9eefc84859c3c8f83b477`.
- `ADR-013` preserves target/compiler and external-product boundaries. SHA-256: `0c9a81262de884a830c484c7cab1e2cc6e543fa21a901fd86ac7b6d0d4d83d86`.

All five ADRs remain accepted and unchanged.
